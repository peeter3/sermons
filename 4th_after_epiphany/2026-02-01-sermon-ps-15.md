# WHO SWEARS TO HIS OWN HURT

Let us pray. *\[Prayer]*  Amen.

Belovèd in the Lord, we have in Psalm 15 a short and sweet psalm of David where we confess the way of living our daily life that is pleasing to God.
> **O LORD, who shall sojourn in Your tent? Who shall dwell on Your holy hill?**

This is the first verse and title of the psalm: **"O LORD, who shall sojourn in your tent?"**  And right away we know the purpose of the psalm: who is pleasing to the Lord? Let's pray about it. Whom does He welcome into His presence? Let's hear from His own lips whom He welcomes in His holy presence. After all, who do you like to be around? Some people more than others. The Lord is the same way: there are some who He likes to be near, and others *\[understatement]*  not so much.

Now though the long psalms, like Psalm 119, are as much God's Word as the short ones, the short ones are easy to remember — they stick to us easily — and this particular psalm, Psalm 15 — five verses in all — is a good one. Keep this simple psalm in our hearts all day long and we *cannot* go wrong.

The psalm continues, **"He who walks blamelessly and does what is right and speaks truth in his heart."**  God is watching us 24/7 — it is found throughout the Bible that He sees our hearts and knows exactly what we're doing. We need only look to Psalm 19 to pray this to the Lord:
> **Who can discern his errors? Declare me innocent from hidden faults.**  (Ps. 19:12)

The Lord sees our hidden faults — our secret sins are laid bare. **"Who can discern his errors?"**  Which of us are even able *to know* that we're sinning? God knows — He sees everything. **"Declare me innocent from hidden faults."**  This we do every time we pray the Lord's Prayer: **"And forgive us our trespasses as we forgive those who trespass against us."**  But that is Psalm 19 and we want to get back to
> **O LORD, who shall sojourn in your tent? … He who walks blamelessly and does what is right and speaks truth in his heart.**

There is definitely truth with the Lord and there is definitely falsehood — the Bible says so. It's not "You have your truth and I have my truth." It's "There is truth and there is error" with the Lord, and there is no end to the error the devil invents to deceive us (John 8:44). The end will finally be when he's thrown in the lake of fire. (Rev. 20:10).

Belovèd in the Lord, Jesus is the Truth:
> **I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me.**  (John 14:6)

This verse, no doubt, comes to mind for many of you, and thank God it does! Maybe this passage from Hebrews, chapter 4 also comes to mind:

\[12] For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart. \[13] And no creature is hidden from his sight, but all are naked and exposed to the eyes of him to whom we must give account. (Heb. 4:12–13)


**The peace of God, which passes all understanding, keep your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus** (Phil. 4:7). Amen.
