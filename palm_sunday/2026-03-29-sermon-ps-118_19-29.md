# THE STONE THAT THE BUILDERS REJECTED HAS BECOME THE CORNERSTONE

Let us pray. *\[Prayer]*  Amen.

Belovèd in the Lord, our psalm for today in not an entire psalm but only the last eleven verses of a psalm, the last verse being **"Oh give thanks to the LORD, for He is good; for His steadfast love endures forever!"**  This does sound familiar, does it not? If you were trained on the King James Bible, you have heard it this way: **"O give thanks unto the LORD; for He is good: for His mercy endureth for ever."**

Take note — and you may have noticed — that *mercy* in the King James is translated *steadfast love* in the English Standard Bible, but behind both words stands a single Hebrew word: *hesed*, which is one of the most important words of the Bible. *Hesed* is a picture into God's heart — *hesed* basically means *God's love*, which is very, very great. So whether you say *steadfast love* or *mercy*, it's the same thing — so wonderful that it is beyond describing, but decribe it we must — believe it we do — live according to our faith in it we do, by the grace of the Spirit.

We behold God's steadfast love put into action as we hear from the Gospels the nonstop mighty works of our Lord Jesus Christ. Today, He rides into Jerusalem on the colt of a donkey.
> **The next day the large crowd that had come to the feast \[of the Passover] heard that Jesus was coming to Jerusalem. So they took branches of palm trees and went out to meet Him, crying out, "Hosanna! Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord, even the King of Israel!" And Jesus found a young donkey and sat on it, just as it is written, "Fear not, daughter of Zion; behold, your King is coming, sitting on a donkey's colt!"**  (John 12:12–15; Zech. 9:9)

Riding in on a donkey's colt? You call that a mighty work? We find that Christ chooses to hide His might in those things that are weak and despised (1 Cor. 1:28), so riding in on a donkey's colt is exactly how He likes it. The crowd cries, *Hosanna*, which means *Help* or *Save, I pray*. This is what the people cry out and this is what our Lord rides into Jerusalem to do — to save us from trouble that no one else able to do. He comes to bear our sins.

The cornerstone of a building bears all the weight. Jesus comes to bear the weight of our sin.

**The peace of God, which passes all understanding, keep your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus** (Phil. 4:7). Amen.
