# BY HIS STEADFAST LOVE IT IS DONE
Let us pray. *\[Prayer]*  Amen.

Belovèd in the Lord, we have before us a most famous phrase from the Bible, **"for His steadfast love endures forever,"**  and the psalm that repeats this phrase over and over. The first line of the psalm is, famously, **"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good, for His steadfast love endures forever."**  We have Psalm 136.

We also have before us today St. Thomas, the apostle — today is his feast day in the church calendar. His moment of fame is today's Holy Gospel.
> **Now Thomas, one of the twelve, called the Twin, was not with them when Jesus came. So the other disciples told him, "We have seen the Lord." But he said to them, "Unless I see in his hands the mark of the nails, and place my finger into the mark of the nails, and place my hand into his side, I will never believe."**  (John 20:24–25)

Thomas is the one to whom our resurrected Lord appeared and *showed him* the wounds in His hands and side. Let this be our first thought on ***His steadfast love endures forever***. Our Lord Jesus Christ, though He was forsaken by His Father on the Cross, bearing our sins (Mark 15:34) — is now alive with a resurrection body — different from His earthly body His resurrection body cannot die, yet on this brand-new body are still seen the wounds of His Crucifixion — the wounds of His death. Christ has conquered death. And we are reminded of what He said:
> **Truly, truly, I say to you, an hour is coming, and is now here, when the dead will hear the voice of the Son of God, and those who hear will live. For as the Father has life in himself, so he has granted the Son also to have life in himself. And he has given him authority to execute judgment, because he is the Son of Man. Do not marvel at this, for an hour is coming when all who are in the tombs will hear his voice and come out, those who have done good to the resurrection of life, and those who have done evil to the resurrection of judgment.**  (John 5:25–29)

By faith in Christ, and by bearing the fruit of faith, the same resurrection of life as that of Jesus is also ours. The enduring love of the Father for us saw the Son through death to the resurrection of life *which now extends even to us* who would have otherwise died in our sins, but who now believe that the Son came from the Father to save us from our sins.

**The peace of God, which passes all understanding, keep your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus** (Phil. 4:7). Amen.
